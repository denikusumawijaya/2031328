Introduction & Real-World Connection

Imagine you are a prehistoric detective opening a high-security cave vault. If you enter the code numbers in the wrong order, the alarm goes off and the cave door locks forever! Math is exactly like that. If we solve a big equation in the wrong order, we get the wrong answer.

Today, we are learning the 'Secret Code' called BODMAS. It tells us exactly which part of the math problem to solve first so we can unlock the right answer every single time. Without BODMAS, math would be chaotic!

Review of Prior Knowledge
00:15 - 00:20

Before we dive into the secret code, look at this simple expression: \( 5 + 2 \times 3 \). If you just read from left to right, you might add first (5+2=7) then multiply (7x3=21). But in math land, multiplication is stronger than addition! We must do multiplication first.

An Operation is a mathematical process like Addition (Plus), Subtraction (Minus), Multiplication (Multiply), and Division (Divide). Today, we will learn the hierarchy—who gets to go first!

Left to Right

\( (5 + 2) \times 3 \)

= \( 7 \times 3 = 21 \)

Multiply First

\( 5 + (2 \times 3) \)

= \( 5 + 6 = 11 \)

Problem Introduction
00:20 - 00:30

Let's meet the secret code: BODMAS. It stands for Brackets (), Orders (squares/roots), Division, Multiplication, Addition, and Subtraction. Think of it as a ladder. We must climb from the top (Brackets) down to the bottom (Subtraction).

If you have both Division and Multiplication, or Addition and Subtraction in the same level, you simply solve them from left to right—just like reading a map!

Look at this: \( (10 + 2) \times 5 \). Based on BODMAS, the brackets are the bosses at the top of the ladder. We must do \( 10 + 2 \) first! What do we get? 12! Then \( 12 \times 5 = 60 \). See how the brackets changed the game?

Guided Practice
00:30 - 00:45

When solving mysteries, writing down your steps is crucial. Let's solve together: \( 50 - (10 + 5) \times 2 \). First, circle the brackets. \( 10 + 5 = 15 \). Write down the new equation: \( 50 - 15 \times 2 \).

Next, look at the BODMAS ladder... do we subtract or multiply first? Correct, Multiply! \( 15 \times 2 = 30 \). Finally, \( 50 - 30 = 20 \). Always rewrite the equation after each step!
